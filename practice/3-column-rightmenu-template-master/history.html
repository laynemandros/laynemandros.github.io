Where was Pi Beta Phi founded?

Pi Beta Phi was founded at Monmouth College, in Monmouth Illinois on April 28th 1867. Pi Beta Phi was originally named I.C. Sorosis and was the first national secret college society to be named using greek litters.

Why was Pi Beta Phi founded?

Pi Beta Phi was founded when twelve women at Monmouth College wanted to benefit from a secret society in the ways that men had benefited from fraternities that had been formed by collegiate men.

Who were Pi Beta Phi's founding sisters?

The twelve founding sisters were:

  • Clara Brownleee Hutchison
  • Libbie Brook Gaddis
  • Emma Brownlee Kilgore
  • Margaret Campbell
  • Rosa Moore
  • Ada Bruen Grier
  • Nancy Black Wallace
  • Jennie Horn Turnbull
  • Jennie Nicol
  • Inez Smith Soule
  • Fannie Thomson
  • Fannie Whitenack Libbey
